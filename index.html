Ph.D. Physics · University of Missouri–Columbia

I grew up in Jhargram, a small town in West Bengal, India. I studied Physics at Midnapore College and went on to complete my M.Sc. at the University of Hyderabad, before spending a year at JNCASR in Bangalore. In 2019, I moved to the University of Missouri–Columbia to pursue a Ph.D. in Theoretical Condensed Matter Physics under Prof. Giovanni Vignale.

My research spans emergent quantum phenomena in two-dimensional materials — from nonsymmorphic Dirac semimetals to twisted bilayer graphene — combining analytical theory with numerical simulation to probe band topology, magneto-optical response, and quantum geometry. I am now building toward hybrid quantum–classical workflows and actively learning machine learning to connect simulation with predictive materials design.

Outside physics, I'm an avid reader — mostly novels — and someone who believes the best ideas live at the edges of disciplines.

Research Vision

What I'm Building

🎯
Long-Term Goal
Generative Hybrid Quantum–Classical Workflow

My overarching goal is to build a modular generative workflow that tightly couples classical simulation, quantum simulation, and machine learning. The idea is to use physics-based quantum and classical solvers to generate high-fidelity training data for correlated quantum systems, then feed those outputs into ML models to construct exact, interpretable predictive models. I'm currently building my ML foundation with this vision in mind — working toward a feedback loop that accelerates the discovery of new quantum materials and device-relevant properties.
