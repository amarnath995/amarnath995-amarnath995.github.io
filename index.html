Amarnath
Chakraborty

Ph.D. Physics · University of Missouri–Columbia

I grew up in Jhargram, a small town in West Bengal, India. I studied Physics at Midnapore College, completed my M.Sc. at the University of Hyderabad, and spent a year at JNCASR, Bangalore before moving to Missouri in 2019 to pursue a Ph.D. under Prof. Giovanni Vignale.

My doctoral work spans emergent quantum phenomena in two-dimensional materials — from nonsymmorphic Dirac semimetals to twisted bilayer graphene — combining analytical theory with numerical simulation to probe band topology, magneto-optical response, and quantum geometry. I'm now working toward hybrid quantum–classical workflows and actively learning machine learning to connect simulation with predictive materials design.

Outside physics, I'm an avid reader — mostly novels — and someone who believes the best ideas live at the edges of disciplines.

Research Vision

What I'm Building

🎯 Long-Term Goal
Generative Hybrid Quantum–Classical Workflow
My overarching goal is to build a modular generative workflow coupling classical simulation, quantum simulation, and machine learning. Physics-based solvers generate high-fidelity training data for correlated quantum systems; those outputs feed ML models to construct exact, interpretable predictive models. I'm building my ML foundation with this vision in mind — working toward a feedback loop that accelerates quantum materials discovery.
